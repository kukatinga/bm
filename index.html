A Leader in Kenyan Handball

Profile Summary

Brian Mathews is a highly respected handball coach and former professional player with extensive experience in the sport. A former captain of the Kenya national handball team, he also played in Denmark's professional leagues before transitioning into coaching.

With a passion for developing young talent and a strategic approach to the game, Brian has become one of the most influential figures in Kenyan handball, shaping the future of the sport at both club and national levels.

Coaching Career

National Team

Current Head Coach of the Kenya National Men's Handball Team, focusing on tactical discipline and enhancing Kenya's competitiveness internationally.

Club Coaching

Head Coach of National Cereals and Produce Board (NCPB) Women's Handball Team, leading them to multiple league titles and championships.

Recognition

Awarded StarTimes/SJAK Coach of the Month in December 2022 for outstanding performance and leadership in Kenyan handball.

Coaching Philosophy

Tactical Discipline

Emphasizing structured play, strategic execution, and adaptability to different game situations. Building teams that can read the game and make smart decisions under pressure.

Youth Development

Investing in the next generation of players through comprehensive training programs, mentorship, and competitive opportunities to nurture Kenya's future handball stars.

Mental Resilience

Developing strong, focused athletes who can maintain peak performance under pressure. Building character and sportsmanship alongside physical skills.

Impact on Kenyan Handball

Beyond his coaching roles, Brian is deeply committed to the growth of handball in Kenya. He dedicates much of his free time mentoring young athletes and nurturing the next generation of handball players.

He holds free handball clinics across the country, offering youth an opportunity to learn the game from an experienced coach and professional player. Brian is passionate about the development of the sport at the grassroots level and believes that structured youth programs are essential for the long-term success of Kenyan handball.

His mentorship and clinics continue to inspire young athletes and contribute to the growth of the sport nationwide, creating pathways for talent development and professional opportunities in handball.
